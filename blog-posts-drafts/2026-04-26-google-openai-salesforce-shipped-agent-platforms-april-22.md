# Google, OpenAI, and Salesforce All Shipped Agent Platforms on April 22 — Here's the Bet We're Refusing to Make for Clients

On April 22, three of the biggest names in enterprise software pushed competing "agent platforms" into production inside 24 hours. OpenAI launched ChatGPT Workspace Agents — shared agents your team builds once and refines together inside ChatGPT and Slack. Google opened Cloud Next 2026 by renaming Vertex AI to the **Gemini Enterprise Agent Platform** and shipping the **A2A (Agent-to-Agent) protocol** to 150 production organizations. Salesforce and Google Cloud announced an expanded partnership wiring Agentforce directly into Gemini.

Three press releases. One day. Same word in every headline: *agents*.

For a small business owner skimming the trade press this week, this looks like good news — more options, more competition. It is not. It's the moment the agent layer fragmented, and the bill for choosing wrong is going to land on SMBs first.

## What Just Changed (In English)

For two years, "agents" mostly meant a chatbot bolted onto your website. As of April 22, agents are something different: software that runs inside your CRM, your inbox, your Slack, and your help desk — *and talks to other agents on your behalf*. Google's A2A protocol exists so a Salesforce agent can hand a task to a Google agent that queries a ServiceNow agent. Anthropic's MCP exists so any client (Claude, Cursor, GHL) can call any tool over a single open connection.

These aren't features. They're the wiring of the next decade of small business software. And the platforms shipping them — Workspace Agents, Agentforce-Gemini, Gemini Enterprise — are not designed for a 12-person services business. They're designed for the IT department of a 12,000-person enterprise.

## The Trend Nobody's Saying Out Loud

The NFIB March Jobs Report came out the same week. Small Business Employment Index down 1.9 points to 101.6. Ten percent of owners now name labor costs as their single biggest problem. Forty-five percent can't find qualified applicants for the roles they're trying to fill.

SMB owners are exactly the buyers who *need* agents, and exactly the buyers being courted with platforms they'll never operate. Agent Gateways, A2A handoffs, Model Armor, zero-trust agent IAM — that's not a stack a roofer or a med spa or a $4M coaching business will run. They'll be sold it anyway. By next quarter, every agency on LinkedIn will brand itself an "Agentforce partner" or "Gemini Enterprise implementer." In 18 months, half of those platforms will be deprecated, rebranded, or absorbed.

## Final Takeaway

The agent layer isn't consolidating. It's fragmenting. April 22 was day one of a multi-year standards war between the largest software companies in the world. The right move for an SMB is not to pick a side — it's to keep your data, your CRM, and your follow-up logic in places that are agnostic to whichever side wins.
