# GHL Quietly Shipped Stripe Tax for 100+ Countries — Your Back Office Is About to Shrink

On April 23, GoHighLevel pushed an update most agencies didn't bother to read. "Global Tax Compliance, powered by Stripe Tax." It quietly added the ability for sub-accounts to configure tax collection, generate tax invoices, and pull tax reports across 100+ countries — from inside the same CRM your team uses to text leads.

The same day, GHL also shipped a small but telling fix: scoped store deletion. Delete a store, and only the store-related pages disappear — not your entire website. Boring on the surface, but the two updates told the same story. The wall between your CRM and your operations stack is gone.

## What Just Changed (In English)

For the last decade, an SMB needed at minimum a CRM, a checkout, a tax engine (Avalara, TaxJar, or your CPA's quarterly cleanup), and a website builder. Each one billed separately. Each one had to be wired together.

After April 23, GHL sub-accounts can collect international tax automatically, generate compliant invoices, and pull tax reports without leaving the CRM. The login most service businesses already pay for now does the work three other tools used to charge for.

This isn't a bolt-on. It's GHL behaving the way HubSpot did at its Spring 2026 Spotlight nine days earlier — when it shipped Smart Deal Progression: AI that listens to a sales call, updates the CRM fields, drafts the follow-up email, and queues the action items, automatically. Same pattern. The CRM is no longer the CRM. It's the operations layer.
